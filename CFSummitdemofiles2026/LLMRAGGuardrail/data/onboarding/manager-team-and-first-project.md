# Manager, Team, and First Project

## Reporting line

Each new hire reports to the hiring manager listed in the offer letter and HR profile. The manager owns role expectations, prioritization, feedback, and performance check-ins. People Ops owns employment records, benefit questions, and onboarding process support.

The onboarding buddy is a peer helper. The buddy can answer practical team questions, explain team norms, and help the employee find the right document or channel. The buddy is not responsible for performance evaluation.

## Team communication

Employees should join their team channel, department channel, company announcements channel, and the new-hire cohort channel during the first three days. Team standups are used for current work, blockers, and handoff risks. Questions that require decisions should be summarized in writing after meetings.

Core collaboration hours are 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM in the employee's local time zone unless the team has a different published norm.

## Role training

Role training includes required learning modules, shadowing, role-specific documentation, and manager-assigned practice tasks. New hires should keep a running list of terms, tools, and decisions that need clarification.

By the end of week one, the employee and manager should agree on first-month outcomes and how progress will be measured.

## First project brief

The first project brief should include the business goal, primary stakeholders, scope, out-of-scope items, success criteria, risks, important links, and expected milestones. The first project should be small enough to complete or meaningfully advance within the first 30 days.

If an employee is blocked, they should tell the manager or buddy within one business day and include the blocker, what they tried, and the decision or access they need.

## Updates

New hires should send a weekly onboarding update during the first month. The update should include work completed, what is next, blockers, questions, and support needed.
