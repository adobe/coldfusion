# Paperwork and Employment Terms

## Day-one paperwork

Day-one paperwork is due by the end of the first business day unless People Ops grants an extension.

Complete these forms:

- Tax withholding
- Direct deposit
- Emergency contact
- Work authorization, where applicable
- Confidentiality acknowledgement
- Code of conduct acknowledgement
- Policy receipt acknowledgement

Review the employment agreement and confidentiality terms before submitting the acknowledgement forms.

Employees should verify preferred name, legal name, home address, personal email, phone number, and emergency contact information in the HR profile.

## Orientation

New hires should attend the day-one welcome session and meet the onboarding coordinator. Bring a government-issued photo ID, any work authorization documents that apply, direct deposit details, emergency contact information, and questions about the onboarding checklist.

If orientation details are missing or the employee cannot attend the scheduled session, they should contact the onboarding coordinator or People Ops before the session starts.

## Confidentiality and company property

Employees must protect confidential information, customer data, source code, business plans, employee records, credentials, and unreleased product information. Company property must be returned at the end of employment or when People Ops requests it.

Employees should not move company confidential information into personal accounts or unmanaged devices.

## Probation and early feedback

The company uses a 90-day introductory period for new hires. The introductory period is used for onboarding support, expectation setting, and early feedback. It does not change at-will employment where applicable.

Managers should hold informal check-ins during weeks 1, 2, and 4. A structured 30-day check-in reviews role clarity, training progress, blockers, and early wins. A 60-day check-in reviews first-project progress. A 90-day check-in confirms ongoing goals and support needs.

## Payroll

Payroll runs twice per month. Direct deposit changes must be submitted at least five business days before payroll close. Employees should contact payroll@example.test for paycheck, tax, or deposit questions.
