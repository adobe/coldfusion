# Benefits and Time Off

## Health coverage

Full-time employees are eligible for medical, dental, and vision coverage. Coverage starts on the first day of the month after the employee's start date. New hires must submit benefit elections within 30 calendar days of their start date. If no election is submitted, the employee will be enrolled in employee-only medical coverage and can change elections at the next open enrollment period.

The company pays 85 percent of the employee-only premium for the standard medical plan. Employees can add dependents at their own premium contribution rate. Dental and vision plans are optional.

## Vacation and holidays

Full-time employees receive 18 days of paid vacation each calendar year, prorated in the first year based on start date. Vacation can be requested after the employee's manager confirms team coverage. Employees should submit planned vacation at least two weeks in advance when possible.

The company observes 11 paid holidays each calendar year. Floating holidays are not carried over after December 31.

## Sick time and leave

Employees receive 8 paid sick days per calendar year, prorated in the first year. Sick time can be used for personal illness, medical appointments, or caring for an immediate family member. Employees should notify their manager as soon as practical.

For medical, parental, bereavement, military, or civic leave, employees should contact People Ops before the leave begins when possible. People Ops will explain documentation, job protection, pay continuation, and return-to-work expectations.

## Retirement plan

Employees can enroll in the retirement plan after 30 days of employment. The company matches 100 percent of the first 3 percent contributed and 50 percent of the next 2 percent contributed. The match is deposited each payroll period after enrollment begins.

Employees should add or update beneficiaries during enrollment and whenever family status changes.

## Benefits questions

Questions about plan details, qualifying life events, missing ID cards, or payroll deductions should go to benefits@example.test. Managers should not collect medical details from employees.
