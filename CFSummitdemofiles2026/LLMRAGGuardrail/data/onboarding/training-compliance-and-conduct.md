# Training, Compliance, and Conduct

## Required training

All new hires must complete security awareness, privacy basics, code of conduct, anti-harassment, workplace safety, and records handling training within the first 14 calendar days. Role-specific training may add modules for customer data, financial approvals, secure development, or people management.

Training completion is tracked in the learning platform. If a required course is missing, the employee should contact learning@example.test.

## Code of conduct

Employees are expected to communicate respectfully, act with integrity, protect confidential information, follow the law, and raise concerns early. Retaliation for good-faith reporting is prohibited.

Conflicts of interest, gifts from vendors, outside work, and public speaking about company matters should be disclosed through the People Ops portal before commitments are made.

## Data handling

Employees should access only the systems and data needed for their role. Customer data, employee records, financial data, and credentials are restricted. Sharing restricted data requires an approved business purpose and an approved channel.

Personal devices should not store restricted company data. Downloaded files should be deleted when no longer needed.

## Expense and procurement basics

Expenses must be submitted within 30 days with an itemized receipt and business purpose. Software subscriptions, contractors, and hardware purchases must go through the procurement process before purchase unless a manager confirms an emergency exception.

## Asking for help

Policy questions can go to People Ops. Security and access questions can go to the IT Service Desk or Security. Role-priority questions should go to the manager.
